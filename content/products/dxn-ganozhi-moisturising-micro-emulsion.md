What is a micro emulsion — and why does it matter for skin?</h2><p id=\"\">‍</p><p id=\"\">A microemulsion is a stable, finely dispersed mix of oil and water phases held together by surfactants. When you add modern delivery science—sometimes referred to as nanoscale encapsulation or nanocarriers—the formula becomes capable of ferrying active ingredients more efficiently into the skin. In plain language: smaller droplets, better delivery.</p><p id=\"\">‍</p><p id=\"\">Why should that interest you? Because the skin is both a barrier and a living organ. Large molecules tend to sit on the surface and provide temporary occlusion. Smaller, well-formed droplets can penetrate deeper into the top layers, where nutrients and repair signals are needed. The DXN Ganozhi Moisturising Micro Emulsion is built on the idea of reducing particle size, stabilizing sensitive botanicals, and supporting targeted absorption so that antioxidants, peptides, and plant extracts can act where they are most effective.</p><p id=\"\">‍</p><p id=\"\">The practical payoff is straightforward: faster absorption, a lighter feel, less residue, and a higher chance that the active ingredients will actually do their job, rather than just decorating the skin’s surface.</p><p id=\"\">‍</p><h2 id=\"\">2. The product in context: how it fits into a routine</h2><p id=\"\">‍</p><p id=\"\">DXN positions this micro emulsion as the final and crucial hydrating step after cleansing and toning. The recommended order is:</p><p id=\"\">‍</p><ol id=\"\"><li id=\"\"><strong id=\"\">Cleanse</strong> — remove dirt, makeup, and excess oil.</li><li id=\"\"><strong id=\"\">Tone</strong> — restore optimal skin pH and sweep away remaining residues.</li><li id=\"\"><strong id=\"\">Micro Emulsion</strong> — delivers moisture and actives deeply; seal the routine.</li></ol><p id=\"\">‍</p><p id=\"\">Applied after a balancing toner, the micro emulsion’s nano-sized molecules encounter a receptive, slightly acidic environment (the skin’s preferred pH). That environment supports enzyme activity and efficient ingredient uptake, which is why toning beforehand enhances the emulsion's performance. Because it’s lightweight, it’s also intended to play nicely under sunscreen and makeup, serving as a hydrating base that doesn’t pill or interfere with application.</p><p id=\"\">‍</p><h2 id=\"\">3. The core technology explained, in plain terms</h2><p id=\"\">‍</p><p id=\"\">The product uses two interlinked principles: micro-emulsion formulation and nanoscale delivery.</p><p id=\"\">‍</p><ul id=\"\"><li id=\"\"><strong id=\"\">Micro-emulsion formulation</strong>: This creates a stable blend where tiny droplets of oil are suspended in water (or vice versa) with the help of mild surfactants. The result is a fluid, silky texture that spreads easily and evaporates without heavy residue.</li><li id=\"\"><strong id=\"\">Nanoscale delivery</strong>: By engineering droplet and carrier sizes at the nanoscale, the emulsion increases the surface area and interaction with skin cells. Smaller particles navigate more efficiently through the skin’s outer layers and deposit actives nearer to where they can influence moisture retention, collagen-producing cells, and antioxidant defenses.</li></ul><p id=\"\">‍</p><p id=\"\">Together, these technologies achieve three useful benefits: they protect delicate botanical compounds from degradation, they enhance delivery reliability, and they maintain a light sensory experience—no greasy film, quick absorption, and a soft finish.</p><p id=\"\">‍</p><h2 id=\"\">4. Key ingredients and their contributions</h2><p id=\"\">‍</p><p id=\"\">A product is more than buzzwords: its ingredients determine the results. The DXN Ganozhi Moisturising Micro Emulsion combines several botanical and nutritional actives chosen to hydrate, calm, protect, and help maintain elasticity.</p><p id=\"\">‍</p><h3 id=\"\">Ganoderma (Reishi) extract — antioxidant and anti-inflammatory support</h3><p id=\"\">‍</p><p id=\"\">Ganoderma lucidum, commonly known as Reishi, is included for its antioxidant constituents and anti-inflammatory terpenoids and polysaccharides. Topical antioxidants help reduce oxidative stress caused by UV exposure and pollution, thereby lowering the burden of free radicals that accelerate aging and damage the skin barrier. Ganoderma supports a calmer surface and helps protect the skin from environmental stressors.</p><p id=\"\">‍</p><h3 id=\"\">Fagus sylvatica (beech bud) extract — firmness and hydration booster.</h3><p id=\"\">‍</p><p id=\"\">Beech bud extract is often used in cosmetics to stimulate components that support firmness, such as collagen synthesis, and to help maintain the skin’s natural moisturizing factors. Over time, it can contribute to improved elasticity and a plumper look by supporting the structural proteins and hydration machinery in the dermis and epidermis.</p><p id=\"\">‍</p><h3 id=\"\">Ginseng extract — revitalizing and anti-aging</h3><p id=\"\">‍</p><p id=\"\">Ginseng is a classic adaptogenic botanical frequently used to boost cellular resilience. In skin care, ginseng extracts are associated with improved skin tone and firmness, supporting microcirculation and metabolic activity in the skin to help it look fresher and more energized.</p><p id=\"\">‍</p><h3 id=\"\">Arnica extract — soothing and calming agent</h3><p id=\"\">‍</p><p id=\"\">Arnica is known for its anti-inflammatory and soothing properties. Topically, it’s used to calm redness, decrease swelling, and support recovery in sensitive or stressed skin. Its inclusion helps reduce reactive irritation and contributes to a more even complexion.</p><p id=\"\">‍</p><h3 id=\"\">Vitamin E — antioxidant and photoprotection cofactor</h3><p id=\"\">‍</p><p id=\"\">Vitamin E (tocopherol) is a well-documented chain-breaking antioxidant. It neutralizes reactive oxygen species and supports skin repair. As a lipid-soluble antioxidant, Vitamin E interacts well with skin barrier lipids and helps protect cell membranes from free radical damage.</p><p id=\"\">‍</p><p id=\"\">Together, these ingredients provide a layered defensive strategy: antioxidants to fight environmental damage, botanicals to support structural proteins and hydration, and soothing agents to manage reactivity.</p><p id=\"\">‍</p><h2 id=\"\">5.
