main_content: "<h3 id=\"\">Causes / Why It Happens (in plain terms)</h3><p>‍</p><ol id=\"\"><li id=\"\"><strong id=\"\">Gradual muscle loss</strong> — without resistance work, muscle mass and strength decline, reducing the calories your body burns at rest.</li><li id=\"\"><strong id=\"\">Less daily activity</strong> — longer commutes, desk jobs, and busy schedules lower incidental movement.</li><li id=\"\"><strong id=\"\">Hormonal drift</strong> — sex hormones and metabolic hormones shift with age and can change appetite and body fat distribution.</li><li id=\"\"><strong id=\"\">Cumulative lifestyle choices</strong> — poorer sleep, more stress, and convenience foods create a metabolic environment that favors fat storage.</li><li id=\"\"><strong id=\"\">Dietary patterns</strong> — skipping breakfast, low protein intake, or frequent sugary/processed foods worsen metabolic flexibility (the ability to switch between burning carbs and fats efficiently).</li></ol><p>‍</p><h3 id=\"\">Solutions / Practical Tips (big-picture + quick wins)</h3><p>‍</p><ul id=\"\"><li id=\"\"><strong id=\"\">Protect muscle with resistance training:</strong> Start with two full-body strength sessions per week and progress to 3 as you adapt. Compound movements (squat, deadlift, push, row) deliver the best return on time.</li><li id=\"\"><strong id=\"\">Prioritize protein:</strong> Aim for a protein target spread across the day (e.g., ~25–35 g protein per meal depending on body size). Protein preserves muscle and increases meal satiety.</li><li id=\"\"><strong id=\"\">Move more, sit less:</strong> Add 20–30 minutes of low-intensity movement daily — brisk walking, active chores, or standing breaks. Small NEAT boosts add meaningful calorie burn.</li><li id=\"\"><strong id=\"\">Improve sleep and reduce stress:</strong> Aim for consistent sleep timing and 7–9 hours nightly. Use short stress routines (breathwork, brief walks) to lower cortisol surges that encourage fat storage.</li><li id=\"\"><strong id=\"\">Choose nutrient-dense foods:</strong> Whole grains, legumes, vegetables, nuts, seeds, lean meats or plant protein, and healthy fats support metabolism and recovery.</li><li id=\"\"><strong id=\"\">Track trends, not daily noise:</strong> Weekly weight, strength progress, and how your clothes fit are better long-term measures than daily scale swings.</li></ul><p>‍</p><h3 id=\"\">Nutrition + Exercise + Lifestyle — The 6-Week Plan (MoF)</h3><p>‍</p><p id=\"\">Below is a structured, practical 6-week plan combining resistance training, nutrition targets, and sensible supplements — designed to be doable and focused on metabolic wins.</p><p>‍</p><p id=\"\"><strong id=\"\">Principles:</strong> progressive overload in strength training, protein-focused meals, daily NEAT, sleep hygiene, and small, consistent habit changes.</p><p>‍</p><p id=\"\"><strong id=\"\">Weeks 1–2 — Foundation</strong></p><p>‍</p><ul id=\"\"><li id=\"\">Strength: 2 full-body sessions/week (bodyweight or light weights).
